Who Are We? Why are we here?

There is an undeniable difference in the way that a major storm impacts people depending on their
wealth. Those who have lower income typically live in buildings that are not as prepared to handle the
powerful forces that are brought about in a natural disaster. Compounded with this, those who make less
money also are significantly less likely to buy luxuries such as flood insurance. Combine the fact that
their homes are less prepared and they are unable to pay for the consequential damage, the difference
in wealth after the situation is even more greatly exaserpated.

That's where we come in. Our goal is to do our best to improve the situation before and after
a disaster strikes. First, insurance for things like flooding, strong winds, earthquakes, etc needs
to be cheaper and more easily accessible. This will help more destitute familes recover from a terrible
catastrophe and get them back on their feet faster. Second, there needs to be better protections in place
to minimize the damage done in the first place. Unfortunately for the people living there, cheaper homes
are typically available in flood zones due to the increased risk. This starts out those who are less well
off on terrible ground (literally and figuratively). Better preparing these areas to be more flood and
weather resistant will greatly aid in halting the growth of the wealth gap before and after disasters.
Most important of all, It will also help keep people safe and out of harm's way as much as possible.
