Who Are We? Why are we here?

We're a small independent not-for-profit dedicated to combating the problem of the rising desparity
between housing. One of the biggest contributing factors to this is the damage caused by natural
disasters. It has been shown especially over the past two decades that after major weather events,
the housing inequality gap widens. People who are affected by these storms can no longer afford
what most of us take for granted. A lot of the times these people are left without a place to eat,
a place to work, a place to call home.

Through our site, we have been able to connect people with the help and resources they need to get back
on their feet. By finding people work and temporary places to stay while they save money, they are
able to get back on the market for a home, instead of being stuck in the cycle of overpriced apartments.
Based on past trends, it is clearly evident that people cannot afford homes after a catastrophic event,
and that trend is only going to continue in the future. With the increasingly intense weather due to
global warming, the desparity between housing is only going to worsen. Our goal is to slow this as
much as possible, and, ideally, reverse the process.
